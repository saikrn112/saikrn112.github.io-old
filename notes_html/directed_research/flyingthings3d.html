I want to download flyingthings3d dataset for optical flow

The FlyingThings3D dataset is a benchmark dataset for optical flow estimation and stereo matching in computer vision. You can download the dataset from the following link:

https://lmb.informatik.uni-freiburg.de/data/FlyingThings3D/FlyingThings3D.zip

Once you download the zip file, extract it to a desired location on your computer. The dataset consists of two parts: "frames_finalpass" and "optical_flow".

The "frames_finalpass" folder contains the original 3D scenes with the rendered frames in png format. The "optical_flow" folder contains the ground truth optical flow estimates for each frame in the "frames_finalpass" folder.

Please note that the dataset is quite large, with a total size of approximately 126 GB. It is recommended that you have a high-speed internet connection and enough disk space available before attempting to download the dataset.

what is clean pass and final pass in it?

In the FlyingThings3D dataset, the "clean pass" and "final pass" are two different stages of the rendering process used to generate the dataset.

The "clean pass" refers to the intermediate rendering stage, where the virtual 3D scene is rendered without any post-processing effects such as motion blur, depth of field, or lens distortion. The resulting frames are sharp and without any visual artifacts, making them suitable for tasks such as stereo matching and optical flow estimation.

The "final pass" refers to the last stage of the rendering process, where the frames are post-processed to add various effects such as motion blur, depth of field, and lens distortion. These effects simulate the characteristics of real-world cameras and produce a more visually appealing result. However, the post-processing effects make the frames unsuitable for tasks such as stereo matching and optical flow estimation, as they introduce artifacts and distortions that can affect the accuracy of the algorithms.

Therefore, in the FlyingThings3D dataset, the "clean pass" frames are used as the input for stereo matching and optical flow algorithms, while the "final pass" frames are provided as a reference for visual inspection and qualitative evaluation of the results.

what is raw data and derived data in it?

In the FlyingThings3D dataset, the "raw data" and "derived data" refer to two different types of data that are included in the dataset.

The "raw data" refers to the original data that was used to create the dataset. This includes the 3D models of the virtual scenes, as well as the parameters used to render the frames, such as camera positions, lighting conditions, and object motion. The raw data also includes the initial renderings of the frames, before any post-processing effects are applied.

In the FlyingThings3D dataset, the raw data is provided in the "3d_models" and "renders_raw" folders, while the derived data is provided in the "optical_flow" and "stereo_matching" folders. The raw data can be used to generate new renderings of the frames with different parameters or post-processing effects, while the derived data can be used to evaluate the performance of different algorithms for optical flow estimation and stereo matching.
